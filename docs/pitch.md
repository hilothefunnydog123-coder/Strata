# Strata — the description

Copy for the site, the deck, the data room, and the 30-second answer to "so what do
you do?" One product, described at every length. All of it stays true to the one thing
that makes Strata Strata: **it tells you how much to trust the answer.**

---

## The one-liner

> **Strata is the evidence engine for medicine — it answers clinical and scientific
> questions from real, graded literature, and tells you exactly how much to trust the
> answer.**

## Taglines (pick one, own it)

- **Evidence-based, honest by design.**
- **The medical AI that grades its own answers.**
- **Answers you can defend.**
- **It shows its work — and its doubt.**
- **Trustable synthesis, not confident guesses.**

## The elevator pitch (≈30 seconds)

> Medicine drowns in literature and starves for answers you can actually trust. The first
> wave of medical AI made that worse — it answers anything, confidently, and cites nothing,
> so it's useless anywhere a wrong answer has consequences. Strata is built to be the
> opposite. Ask it a clinical or scientific question and it searches real published
> evidence, places every source on the evidence pyramid — meta-analysis down to case
> report — and hands back an answer anchored to those papers with an honest verdict on how
> strong the backing actually is. It never invents a fact, and when the evidence is thin,
> it says so. We start with the teams whose whole job is defensible evidence — pharma
> medical affairs, payers, systematic reviewers — and become the trust layer the rest of
> health AI is built on.

## The paragraph (for the site)

> **Strata is a medical evidence engine that grades the strength of its own answers.** Ask
> a clinical question and Strata searches real published literature, places every source on
> the evidence pyramid — meta-analysis, randomized trial, cohort, case report — and returns
> an answer with an honest verdict on how strong the evidence actually is: strong, moderate,
> weak, or absent. The thing that makes AI dangerous in medicine is confident hallucination.
> Strata is engineered to be the opposite: it never invents a fact — every claim is anchored
> to a specific paper, with a citation and a link — and it tells you plainly when the
> literature doesn't settle the question. Not a chatbot that will say anything. A second
> opinion that shows its work, and its doubt.

## Why it's different (three lines)

- **Real sources only.** Every answer is anchored to specific papers, with IDs and links.
  No paper, no claim.
- **Graded, not just cited.** A citation isn't evidence — a *good* one is. Strata classifies
  each study's design and rank so a lone case report never gets to sound like settled science.
- **Honest about strength.** Every answer carries a computed verdict from the quality,
  quantity, size, and recency of what it found. If the literature is silent, it says so.

## Positioning statement (for the deck)

> **For** the teams who live or die by defensible evidence — medical affairs, market access,
> payers, guideline and systematic-review groups — **who** need to turn an overwhelming,
> ever-growing literature into answers they can stand behind, **Strata is** an evidence-
> intelligence platform **that** delivers grounded, graded, fully-cited synthesis with an
> audit trail. **Unlike** medical chatbots that answer confidently and cite nothing, **Strata**
> shows every source, grades how strong it is, and never pretends to know more than the
> evidence supports.

## The narrative (for the memo / the room)

> Every medical decision is, underneath, an evidence problem: find the best available
> studies, judge how good they are, and act. That has quietly become impossible to do well
> by hand — millions of new papers a year, systematic reviews that take a year and cost a
> fortune, clinicians who get minutes. AI was supposed to fix it. Instead the first wave
> optimized for fluency and produced confident fabrication — the one failure mode a regulated,
> liable, life-and-death field cannot tolerate.
>
> Strata takes the opposite bet: that in medicine the scarce, valuable thing is not another
> answer but a *trustable* one. So Strata is grounded (the answer comes only from retrieved
> papers), graded (every source ranked on the evidence pyramid, the whole body given a
> strength verdict), and honest (silent evidence is reported as silence, not filled from
> imagination). It sells trust, not answers.
>
> We start where trust is already the culture and the budget is real — life-sciences evidence
> and medical-affairs teams — collapsing months of systematic review and medical-information
> work into days, with a full citation audit trail. From there Strata compounds into an
> expert-graded evidence graph and, ultimately, the grounding-and-grading layer that the rest
> of health AI embeds instead of rebuilds. The moat isn't the retrieval; it's the graded graph
> that grows with every question and the calibration that gets sharper with every expert
> correction.
>
> The promise to the market is the same as the promise in every answer: we will show you the
> evidence, we will tell you how strong it is, and we will never pretend to know more than we
> do.

## Words we own / words we avoid

**Own:** trustable, grounded, graded, honest, defensible, evidence, provenance, calibrated,
audit trail, second opinion.
**Avoid:** *diagnose, cure, treat, advice, replace your doctor, guarantees* — Strata is
decision support and reference, not medical advice, and the copy should never blur that line.
